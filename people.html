Our People

Dr. Braatz's research is in applied mathematics and control theory and its application to manufacturing systems where the control of events at the molecular scale is key to product quality. For more information, see Dr. Braatz's group page.

Dr. Gopaluni's primary research interests are in Process Modeling and Control. He is interested in developing and studying the properties of data-based models for a variety of chemical and biological systems. These systems are typically nonlinear, hybrid and time varying, and their measurements are often noisy and irregular. The thrust of his research is on developing high quality models for such processes and adapting them to reflect process changes. His research interests also span a wide range of other areas including identification for control, model predictive controllers, adaptive control, and performance monitoring and assessment. For more information, see Dr. Gopaluni's page.

Yiting has finished both his BASc and MASc degrees at CHBE. His interests are process control and statistical modeelling of time-series data. His current PhD research focuses on the application of Machine Learning techniques to design smart controllers, which identify and predict process faults ahead of time and apply appropriate control actions to prevent such faults.
